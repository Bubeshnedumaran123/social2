The Red Fort

What is Red fort?

the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan built the Red Fort in Delhi in 1638. The fort was designed by architect Ustad Ahmad Lahauri. It took 8 years and 10 months to build. Shah Jahan was the fifth Mughal emperor. He commissioned the fort to serve as the palace for his capital Shahjahanabad. He moved his capital from Agra to Delhi. The fort was built on the banks of the Yamuna River, whose water filled the fort's moats. The Red Fort was the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years. The last Mughal emperor, Bahadur Shah Zafar, was crowned at the Red Fort on September 29, 1837.

Some Info about Red Fort.

*It's a UNESCO World Heritage Site. *It's octagonal in shape and took about ten years to build. *It's located on 256 acres of land. *It features a mix of architectural styles, including Islamic, Hindi, Timurid, and Persian. *It's named after its 2.5-km-long enclosing walls made of red sandstone. *It was built in 1648 by Shah Jahan as the palace fort of Shahjahanabad, the new capital of the fifth Mughal Emperor of India. *The British Government painted the fort red after the white stone started chipping off. *The prime minister of India unfurls the national flag at the Red Fort every year on independence day. *The Koh-i-Noor diamond belonged to the Red Fort's Royal Family. *The last Mughal Emperor, Bahadur Shah Zafar, was tried by the British for treason in the Red Fort. *The joint court-martial of officers of Subhash Chandra Bose's Indian National Army was held at the Red Fort. *The Red Fort has a light and sound show. *The Red Fort's most famous structures include the Hall of Public Audience and the Hall of Private Audience.
